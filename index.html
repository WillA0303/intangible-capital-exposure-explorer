Help & methodology

This explorer uses a static, illustrative dataset of large global companies to show how different business models can have very different exposure to intangible capital.

What is intangible capital?

  • Accounting intangibles: software, patents, trademarks, brands and goodwill recorded on the balance sheet.
  • Intangible investment: R&D, software development, data, and brand building spend that often goes through the income statement rather than the balance sheet.

Traditional accounting treats most intangible investment as an expense rather than an asset, which can distort measures like return on capital and reported profitability. The goal here is not to fix accounting, but to provide a compact view of which companies lean most heavily on intangibles.

Metrics used

  • Intangible assets % of assets: accounting intangibles plus goodwill as a share of total assets.
  • Goodwill % of assets: goodwill as a share of total assets.
  • R&D % of sales: research and development as a share of revenue.
  • Marketing % of sales: sales, marketing and brand spend as a share of revenue.
  • Software/services tilt: a rough 0-100 score indicating how software or service heavy the model is vs physical assets.

Intangible exposure score

For each company the tool constructs a simple index from 0 to 100 based on:

  • Intangible assets as a share of assets
  • R&D and marketing intensity
  • Software/services tilt

The score is then grouped into tiers:

  • 0 - 33: Low intangible
  • 34 - 66: Medium intangible
  • 67 - 100: High intangible

Important limitations

Company metrics in this demo are approximate, static and illustrative only. They are not up to date, not guaranteed to be accurate, and not suitable for making investment decisions. This tool is for educational use only and does not constitute investment advice.
