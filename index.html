Help & Framework

This tool helps you visualise how efficiently companies deploy capital. Each company is plotted by its ROIC (return on invested capital) on the vertical axis and its reinvestment rate on the horizontal axis.

  • ROIC measures how much after-tax operating profit a company earns for each unit of capital it uses.
  • Reinvestment rate approximates how much of that profit is reinvested to grow the capital base.
  • Cost of capital is the hurdle rate a company needs to exceed to create value.

The four quadrants are interpreted as:

  • Compounders: high ROIC and high reinvestment – potentially powerful engines of long-term value creation.
  • Cash generators: high ROIC but low reinvestment – may produce strong cash but limited growth.
  • Mature stabilisers: low ROIC and low reinvestment – often ex-growth or more defensive profiles.
  • Value traps / capital burners: low ROIC but high reinvestment – growth that risks destroying value.

All inputs are approximate and based on your own analysis (e.g. using annual reports, investor presentations, or your models). The toy 5-year scenario uses a simple capital base of 100 and shows how reinvestment at the chosen ROIC might compound.
